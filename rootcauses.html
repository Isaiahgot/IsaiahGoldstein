Root Cause #1:

Urban environments are built to discourage bikes and cycling altogether.

Root Cause #2:

cycling isn’t equitable for a large number of people, it is dominated be white men in most scenarios.


Evidence #1
  • In Urban cities like London, Cyclists are more likely to get in accidents with cars than any other type of vehicle. (Rachel Aldered)

    • "Does More Cycling Mean More Diversity in Cycling?"

Evidence #2
  • In other cities like Dublin, Ireland, the lack of bike paths and safe areas for people to commute on bikes cause the rate at which people cycle to decrease by 24%. (Rachel Aldered)

    • "Does More Cycling Mean More Diversity in Cycling?"

Evidence #1
  • “For example, in 2011 in the local authority of Adur the percentage of females among cyclist commuters was 27.7% and the percentage of females among all commuters was 48.7%, giving a ratio of 27.7/48.7 = 0.57”

    • "Why we need to champion diversity in cycling"

Evidence #2
  • “As cycling increases, cultural norms may take time to change and hold back changes in gender equity. For example, while a more ‘female-friendly’ cycling environment … could attract some women as ‘early adopters’, others may take longer to consider cycling, perhaps being prompted to try it after hearing about it from female colleagues or friends”

    • "Does More Cycling Mean More Diversity in Cycling?"


Explanation #1
  • When there is a high risk of crashes between bikes and cars, people get discouraged from biking or cycling for pleasure or to commute as a means to an end. In addition, Parents dont feel the need to teach their children to ride bikes if there is a high possibility that their child could get hurt, which is even worse because roughly 35 percent of all London children cannot comfortably ride a bike. This is worse in the long run.
Explanation #2
  • Like Accidents, when there is a lack of safety regarding bikes, people tend to not want to bike. Having bike lanes is important in fostering safety in communities and promoting the idea that cycling is healthy, safe, and can be a benefiting factor for connecting to others.
Explanation #1
  • cycling, or cycling as a means to an end for commuting is a majority male activity. ⅔ of all cyclist commuters are male, this needs to change in order to get more people on bikes.
Explanation #2
  • The idea of women in cycling is very important and making cycling significantly more female friendly is necessary in this day and age. But, The cycling industry as well as cities don’t feel like they have the time or money for anything to change

Implications #1
  • An increase in the safety of cyclists and the infrastructure around cycling is extremely important to the cycling community. Both increasing awareness of bikes from a driver's perspective and creating more bike friendly paths will help grow the industry and the communities of cyclists in each city.
Implications #2
  • Increasing the types of people who cycle and where they do it will help show the world that cycling is for everyone. This also helps grow the industry and the communities that surround cycling in cities or other urban areas.
